Early Life

Mahendra Singh Dhoni was born on July 7, 1981 in Ranchi, Jharkhand, India. Before becoming a cricketer, he was very interested in football and badminton. Dhoni worked as a ticket collector at Kharagpur railway station for a few years. Later, he focused completely on cricket and soon became one of the most successful players in the world.

Debut

MS Dhoni made his international debut in 2004 in an ODI match against Bangladesh. He played his first Test match in 2005 against Sri Lanka and made his T20 International debut in 2006 against South Africa. His aggressive batting style and powerful finishing made him popular among cricket fans.

Career Highlights

  • Former captain of the Indian cricket team.
  • Known as one of the best finishers in cricket history.
  • Led India to victory in the 2007 ICC T20 World Cup.
  • Led India to victory in the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup.
  • Led India to victory in the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy.
  • Captain of Chennai Super Kings in the IPL.
  • One of the greatest wicketkeeper-batsmen in cricket.

Captaincy

MS Dhoni is considered one of the most successful captains in cricket history. Under his leadership, India achieved the number one ranking in Test cricket. He was known for his calm attitude on the field and his ability to make smart decisions under pressure. Dhoni led India in all formats of the game and won multiple ICC trophies.

Awards

  • Padma Bhushan – 2018
  • Padma Shri – 2009
  • Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna – 2007
  • ICC ODI Player of the Year – 2008 and 2009
  • Wisden Leading Cricketer in the World – 2008

Marriage Life

MS Dhoni married Sakshi Singh Rawat on July 4, 2010. Their wedding was a private ceremony attended by close family and friends. The couple has a daughter named Ziva Dhoni who was born in 2015. Dhoni is known for keeping his personal life simple and spending time with his family.

Achievements

MS Dhoni is the only captain in cricket history to win all three ICC trophies (T20 World Cup, Cricket World Cup, and Champions Trophy). He is also known for his famous "helicopter shot". Dhoni retired from international cricket in 2020 but continues to play in the IPL.
